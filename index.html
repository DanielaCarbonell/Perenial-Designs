Who We Are

After graduating from the University of Cranfield in the UK in 1996, with a degree in environmental managmment, Jen was hired as a gardener at Swiss Gardens, one of the major historic gardens in Bedfordshire, England. Here, she learned how to deadhead rhododendrons, a skill and necessity that has never left her. She went on to designing other people's gardens, as well as her own, in the UK, where gardening is a national pastime and where she learned her skills. On moving back to the US, she began to design gardens in Maine, small and large, and has been working for the last 25 years on every type of garden, from cottages to castles. Paul, who never used to grow anything that couldn't be eaten, joined her in designing and implementing gardens when the pandemic hit and his journalism jobs dried up. They have been working together for the last six years, and now even he knows the difference between a rhododendron and an azalea.
